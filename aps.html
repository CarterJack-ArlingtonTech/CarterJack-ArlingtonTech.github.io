30 Seniors Named National Merit Scholarship Semifinalists

Earlier this week, the National Merit Scholarship Program announced that 29 Arlington students are semifinalists in the 71st annual National Merit Scholarship Competition.

Community Celebrates 100th Anniversary of Historic John M. Langston School

The John M. Langston Citizens Association (JMLCA) and Arlington Public Schools (APS) invite the community to join a special centennial celebration honoring the 100th anniversary of the John M. Langston School, which opened its doors on November 7, 1925. 

Arlington Public Schools Launches Pilot Readers Rise Program to Inspire a Love of Reading

APS, in partnership with community volunteers, is proud to announce the launch of Readers Rise: Empowering Young Minds Through Reading — an initiative designed to foster a love of reading, strengthen literacy skills, and build lasting mentorship connections for students.

School Board Member Zuraya Tapia-Hadley to Host Open Office Hours on Mon, Sept. 8

School Board Member Zuraya Tapia-Hadley will hold in-person Open Office Hours on Mon, Sept. 8, from 6-8 p.m. at the Syphax Educational Center.

Save the Date for 2025 Back-to-School Nights

We are excited to announce our annual Back-to-School Nights! This is an opportunity to step into your student’s world, meet their teachers, and get a sneak peek into what to expect this year.

Arlington Public Schools Seeks Federal Court’s Relief to Unfreeze Federal Funds

We are providing an update about our school division’s designation by the U.S. Department of Education as a high-risk grantee, effectively freezing $23 million in funding APS relies on to provide services to students and families.
